A real flow — venue inquiry, end to end

From "How much for an October Saturday?" to a sent reply, in 6 minutes.

Click any step to see what's happening underneath. Before MasCom, this took 35 minutes of manual reading, looking up calendars, drafting, checking. Now it's six.

35 minutes manual → 6 minutes total · 4 min draft + 2 min Paul reviews/sends
01
Inquiry arrives at the dedicated venue inbox
Producer · email-to-drive skill
A bride emails the venue. Within seconds the email is captured, parsed, and filed into the right folder in cloud storage — tagged by date and source. No manual filing, no inbox sprawl. The system knows this is a venue inquiry because of who it was sent to and the keywords inside.
+
02
Triage decides what kind of work this is
Producer · Intake (the front-door AI)
The lightest, fastest AI triages the request: what is this, what does it need, who should handle it. It drafts a Session Brief — a one-page summary that gets passed up the chain — and identifies which custom skill should fire next.
+
03
The right specialist skill activates
Producer · venue-inquiry skill
The skill pulls in the venue's current pricing, availability calendar, and FAQ responses. It composes a warm, on-brand response — not a template, but a message that addresses her specific dates, mentions tour times, and includes a booking link. This is where the work happens.
+
04
Quality check — nothing ships without it
Gate · Quality Auditor (QAC)
Grammar. Tone. Brand voice. Link accuracy. PII scan to confirm no sensitive data leaked. If anything fails, the draft goes back to be rewritten. No exceptions, no shortcuts. This gate is what makes it trustworthy enough to put in front of customers.
+
05
Draft lands in your inbox for sign-off
Producer · Junior (the filer)
The draft is created as a saved email draft. The human (Paul) reviews, makes any personal touches, hits send. Two minutes of his time. Meanwhile, a 30-day follow-up is automatically scheduled — if she tours, she gets a check-in. If she books, a review request is queued.
+
06
Session state is logged to the ledger
Producer · Junior · append-only history
Every action — who triaged, what skill fired, what was approved, what shipped — is appended to the session-state log. Append-only. Cannot be rewritten. If something goes wrong six months from now, the audit trail is right there.

The Team — five tiers

Five tiers, one chain. Everything passes through the gate.

Every task — from "send this venue reply" to "audit our advertising infrastructure" — flows through the same chain. Light AI for triage. Heavy AI for strategy. A quality gate before anything reaches the human. The roles map cleanly across whatever AI you bring.

ITC
Intake
First touch · fastest model
The front door. New request arrives, intake triages: what is this, what does it need, who should handle it. Drafts a session brief, passes upward.
MRC
Master
Senior strategist · highest tier
Reads the brief, reads memory, claims the role, builds the plan. Reserved for decisions that matter — strategy, devil's advocate, infrastructure debugging.
SRC
Senior
Executor · drafts deliverables
Takes the plan, builds the draft, runs the audit, fixes the infrastructure. The heavy lifting. Most actual deliverables originate here.
QAC
Quality
Gate · nothing ships without it
Grammar, accuracy, brand voice, link integrity, PII scan. Every artifact passes through. Failures route back; only clean work reaches the human.
JRC
Junior
Filer · session state · ledger
Once work passes QA, junior files it to the right folder, appends the session state, presents the deliverable with a clean link to the human.

Why this exists — a brief story

The Restoration Architecture.

MasCom did not start as a software project. It started as a working system one person built to keep a real venue running, real properties operating, and real customers heard — without hiring out roles he could not afford to fill. He restores buildings as a literal job. He built MasCom so the systems behind those buildings could be restored too.

"The buildings were broken. The systems were broken. All have been rebuilt from the foundation up. That is not a coincidence. That is architecture." — from the build journal
Buildings
Four properties under restoration. One flagship venue open and operating: Main Street Manor in Crittenden, Kentucky.
Systems
22 custom skills, 8 SOP folders, a 5-tier role cascade, a forensic-scored ledger of every decision. Built session by session.
Discipline
Append-only history. ROI gate before each escalation. Quality check before anything ships. The work is holding.

MasCom is, at its core, a system of organized hope: that if you build the right structure, put the right guardrails in place, and refuse to cut corners even when you're exhausted — the work will hold. Now anyone with a real role and a real workload can run their own.
