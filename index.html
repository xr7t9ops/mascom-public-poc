Multi-AI Council Room

Stew talks to all your AIs at once. Picks the best one for each question.

Most AI tools give you one model. Stew uses a council. For every task, Stew routes to the AI best suited for it — at the price tier you're paying. The routing intelligence is the moat.

What you're seeing: a single project routed across the council. Junior #1 went to a fast, cheap model (free-tier-default) because the task was simple summarization. Junior #2 went to a deep-reasoning model because it required structured legal analysis. Junior #3 went to a different provider entirely because that provider is best at this category right now. Quality compared all four results adversarially before any answer reached you.

Free tier: the council uses bundled fast models. Paid tiers: the council uses your connected AIs (bring your own keys) and routes to the strongest one for each task class — never the most expensive by default.

Why Stew Exists

To help every single person in the world.

The world has tiny problems and massive ones. The same AI tools should be able to help with both — and they should belong to the person, not the platform. Stew exists because that wasn't true yet.

The tiny problems matter.

Where did I park the car? Did I send Aunt Carol a thank-you? What was the Wi-Fi password at the rental? Stew remembers the small things so you don't have to.

The massive problems are organizable.

A multi-property real-estate portfolio, a regulated practice with hundreds of clients, a research project that spans years. The pattern is the same: organized projects, audited decisions, confidence scored.

The architecture should belong to you.

Your documents stay in your cloud. Your AI keys stay yours. Your decisions stay yours by name. Stew is the helper. You are the author.

The principles are universal.

Linked everything. Confidence on every conclusion. Adversarial audit. Auditable trail. These work whether you're managing a household or a Fortune-equivalent operation.
